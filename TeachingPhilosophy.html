Alice Springs Mparntwe Education Declaration

TAS disciplines allow students to practise skills and acquire information that are essential for all students to have and utilise in all parts of their lives and become confident and creative individuals.

A TAS educator has the responsibility to provide opportunities for young people to prepare for independence by providing them the skills and knowledge needed to live a healthy lifestyle, which they may then pass on to their families or apply to future employment.

Thus promoting that young people become more engaged and knowledgeable members of society.

Teaching Philosophy

I hope this page informs you about my Teaching Philosophy

TAS is an area of study where students solve problems with creativity with both practical and hypothetical solutions. As a future teacher, I will attempt to emulate a safe and welcoming environment where students can feel comfortable and learn. TAS subjects allow for students to strive and create high standard outcomes even if they struggle in other subjects.

My Experience has influenced my Philosophy

Due to my experience as a tutor and an SLSO, I believe one of my greatest strengths is my confidence in the classroom. I am very comfortable talking in front of the class and am constantly improving my voice projection. These roles have also helped me to conceptualise ideas in the way of learners and learned the importance of teaching concepts in different ways. This is extremely important in TAS, when teaching content and practical skills, educators must consider diverse learning needs and make alterations to best suit the learner.

Teacher knowledge is extremely important for student academic success. A fundamental understanding of pedagogical processes and comprehension of content area do not necessarily ensure high outcomes for students however have a stronger correlation to higher marks and better quality work.
