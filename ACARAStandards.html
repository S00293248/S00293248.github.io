Importance of Numeracy in Food Technology

Literacy and Numeracy are integral facets of learning in the Food Technology Curriculum. It is important to consider the needs of diverse learners and attempt to cater for these whilst providing a cohesive lesson. Combining General Capability skills with content from the course creates lifelong learners that have skills and knowledge adaptable to other areas of learning.

ACARA Standards

These standards are very important to for all teachers in Australia

Understanding the effects of multiplication and division with decimals (OwD3).

This numeracy skill develops students' competency to complete equations involving multiplication, division and fractions (ACARA, 2020). This skill directly relates to FT5-11, detailing the importance of planning and preparing food for specific purposes (NESA, 2019). This could include a classroom activity to modify a recipe and use multiplication and division to convert the measurements to a suitable amount of the intended outcome. This could also include using fractions to change cup or spoon measures to a smaller or larger amount and simplifying these new numbers.

Writes to explain and analyse (CrT11)

Food Technology is a subject that requires students to explain and analyse concepts whilst using accurate vocabulary to understand ideas and develop writing skills which is outlined in CrT11 (ACARA, 2020). This is related to the outcome FT5-3, which requires students to explain the physical and chemical aspects of a variety of foods (NESA, 2019). This could be integrated into lessons involving writing activities for students to describe these aspects and demonstrate their knowledge.

Understanding Texts (UnT9)

Understanding texts outlines how a learner advances their capacity to comprehend, employ, engage with and evaluate texts to construct their meaning (ACARA, 2020). This connects to FT4-8, the skill of collecting and using information and FT4-13 in which students must acknowledge the effects that food and connected practices have on society (NESA, 2019). An activity that could be implemented would be reading articles from different perspectives and observing the effects on individuals, the community, and the environment.
